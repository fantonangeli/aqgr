Conservation and sustainable use of AqGR (P2)

Conservation and sustainable use of AqGR for: {{countryName}} (P2)

Whilst currently all farmed AqGR still exist as wild relatives, some AqGR are considered under threat. Both wild relatives and farmed types of AqGR represent a key resource for the future development of aquaculture and need to be conserved. At the name time, aquaculture and farmed AqGR can represent a risk to biodiversity. Such disruption to biodiversity can occur through habitat disruption brought about by aquaculture, by disease introduced from cultured species and through genetic contamination from escapes or deliberate introductions of farmed AqGR into the wild.

This page summarises information contained in the Registry relating to the risks and controls associated with farmed AqGR and also summarises the in situ and ex situ conservation resources (protected areas, live and in vitro gene banks) for AqGR which exist in member countries.
